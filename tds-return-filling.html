What is TDS and who are required to comply with it.

TDS is an abbreviation for Tax Deducted at Source. As per the Income Tax Act, every company, partnership firm, LLP, Trust and Individual who is liable for a tax audit is required to deduct a certain percentage from the payment if the same exceeds the limits given by the government. This deducted amount should be deposited to the government monthly.

The company or person making the following type of payments is required to deduct TDS on payments which include: Salary, Commission, Professional Fees, Technical Fees, Rent, Consultation Fees, Interest, Foreign Payments, Advertisement Fees, Contractor Fees, etc. In case of non-deduction of TDS government can disallow the expenses and issue the notice for default in TDS compliance.

Documents required to file the TDS Return

  • Invoice of the service provider/ supplier.
  • Details of Last TDS return filled if any.
  • Copy of PAN and TAN.
  • Details of salaried employees: Payment details, KYC and deductions.
  • TRACES and E-Filling user ID details if any.

WHY SHOULD YOU TIMELY FILE YOUR TDS RETURNS?

To avoid disallowance of the expenditure:

In case if the person who is supposed to file the TDS return and deduct TDS fails to do so on time, then the Income Tax Department shall disallow the expenditure on which the TDS was required to be deducted. Since TDS is required to be deducted in the majority of expenditure, the non-compliance of the same may result in disallowance of all such expenses.

To avoid the fine:

A heavy late filing fee of INR 200/- per day is levied in case of late filing of the TDS/TCS return. Additionally, an interest of 1% to 1.5% per month of the TDS amount is levied in case of delay in deduction or deposit of TDS to government.

To prevent additional penalty:

Besides the minimum late filing fees of INR 200, if there is a delay in filing TDS return for one year then an individual or company would be liable for the additional penalty and prosecution. This penalty ranges from INR 10,000 to INR 1 lakh.

To facilitate the reconciliation of the ITR:

TDS deducted by the deductor is reflected in the 26AS of the deductee in the respective financial year. The same is useful to calculate the total Income, especially the case of individuals.

Frequently Asked Questions

WHAT IS THE THRESHOLD LIMMIT FOR TDS EDUCATION?

The person who deducts TDS is responsible for furnishing the TDS Return. It is mandatory to file TDS Return within time; otherwise, they are liable to pay the penalty for failing to submit the e-TDS Return.

  • The following assesses have to file TDS return quarterly if they are:
  • Individual and HUF whose accounts are Audited
  • A Private Limited Company, OPC and Limited Company.
  • Partnership Firm and LLP.
  • Trust and Association of Person.
  • Local Authorities and Government Authority.
WHO ARE REQUIRED TO FILE TDS RETURNS?

In respect of various payments that are liable for a deduction, the Income Tax department has specified a limit till which no TDS is required to be deducted. The threshold limit is different for different sections of Income Tax.

WHO IS REQUIRED TO DEDUCT TDS IN CASE OF FOREIGN PAYMENTS?

All types of entity are required to deduct TDS in case the payment is made to a foreigner whether there is a requirement to do tax audit or not.

WHAT ARE THE CASES IN WHICH NON-AUDIT INDIVIDUALS ARE REQUIRED TO DEDUCT TDS?

Non audit Individuals are required to deduct TDS in case of following types of payment:

  1. Payment of more than INR 50 Lakhs to any Contractor or professional: Sec 194M.
  2. Payment of rent of more than INR 50,000 or above per month: Sec 194 IB.
  3. Payment of more than INR 50 Lakhs or above for purchase of property: Sec 194 IA.
  4. Any payment to NRI: Sec 195.

WHAT IS TAX DEDUCTED AT SOURCE?

For an easy and efficient collection of taxes, the Income Tax department of India has incorporated a system of deduction of tax at the point of generation of the income. This system is known as Tax Deducted at Source or TDS. Under this system, tax is deducted on receiving income and the same is deposited to the department of Income Tax.

WHAT IS THE RATE OF TDS IN CASE OF NON-RESIDENTS?

Rate of TDS in case of the non-resident shall be the rate prevailing in the Income Tax Act or as per DTAA of that country whichever is lower.

IS INVOICE FOR THE SUPPLIER MANDATORY FOR DEDUCTION OF TDS?

No, it is not mandatory, but you should have his PAN card number. In case if there is no PAN card, then the rate of TDS shall be 20%.

HOW TO DEPOSIT TDS WITH BANK?

Payment can be made using net banking online on NSDL by selecting Challan 281. The TDS payments should be made before filing the TDS return. E-payment is compulsory for all Corporate assesses and non-corporate assessees who are liable for audit under section 44AB. Physical payment can be made using Challan 281 in an authorized bank branch.

IS TDS REQUIRED TO BE DEDUCTED ON THE AMOUNT OF GST?

No TDS is not required to be deducted on the amount of GST that is being paid. This is because the amount of GST is not the Income of the assesse as the same has to be remitted to the government.

WHAT IS THE TDS CERTIFICATE AND WHEN IT CAN BE ISSUED?

TDS certificate is proof that the deductor has deducted the TDS and has deposited the same to the government. Deductor at the end of every year is required to issue the certificate to all the entities whose TDS has been deducted.

WHAT IS THE RATE OF TDS IN CASE OF PAN IS NOT PROVIDED BY THE PERSON?

The rate of TDS in case of residential individual shall be the rate applicable for the deduction or 20% whichever is higher.

WHAT ARE THINGS TO BE TAKEN CARE WHILE FILING TDS RETURN?

While filling TDS return the most important thing is to assure that PAN of the person is correctly filled as a mistake in the same will result in passing the TDS credit to a wrong person. However if you avail service with us, then the same shall be taken care by our team.

WHAT IS A LOWER DEDUCTION UNDER TDS?

In case if the net taxable income of the Individual is lower than the amount of tax payable by him then under such case, the Individual shall obtain a lower deduction certificate from the Income Tax department.
